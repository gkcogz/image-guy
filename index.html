Quick Format Guide

JPEG (.jpg)

Best for: Photographs and complex images.
Provides the smallest file size with lossy compression, sacrificing a tiny amount of quality for a huge reduction in size.

PNG

Best for: Logos, graphics, and images requiring a transparent background.
Offers lossless compression, meaning perfect quality is preserved, but results in larger file sizes.

WebP

Best for: A modern replacement for both JPG and PNG.
Provides superior compression for both lossy and lossless images, resulting in smaller files at the same quality level.
